Diva Derby

  • back to Design Technology Department Homepage

 

 

The Diva Derby is our Design and Technology department’s event involving 40 Yr. 9 female pupils verses 40 Yr. 9 male pupils to design and make their own radio controlled cars. The challenge is to make the cars out of cardboard and then to race them around a specially designed track held in the Dutch Barn.

The reasons why the Diva Derby was created are:

  • To increase the uptake of girls in Design and Technology. Girls are fantastic. The government is crying out for girls to take a DT topic as an option due to the lack of females taking Engineering as a career path due to the stigma that is still attached to DT that it is a “boys only” subject. The DT department at Whitchurch are changing that seeing their biggest ever uptake of girls taking a GCSE DT subject.
  • To teach about ESDGC, sustainability and the awareness of materials used.
  • The importance of numeracy and STEM subjects.
  • For the pupils to have fun and realise how vital technology is in the world that we live in.

Making this year extra special once again was the involvement of the very prestigious design and automotive engineering company……. Aston Martin!

Mr Mark Trinder – Technical Trainer and their two apprentices Jack and Tamsin from Aston Martin arrived in their V12 6 litre Rapide wowing the pupils and the staff!

The team at Aston Martin had another ace up their sleeve. This year’s overall girl and boy champions will get to be invited to have a tour of Aston Martin Head Quarters in Warick. 

Our pupils were split into eight groups of girls and eight groups of boys and had two hours to design, make and test their cars. After the two hours were up, it was race time!

Our pupils were fantastic and came up with some outstanding and creative design solutions.
To win the Diva Derby, the Girls and Boys teams would have to be within the top two fastest from their heats and then be entered into their respective finals. The winner of the Girls final would then battle it out with the winners of the Boys final to decide who would be crowned the Ultimate Champions!

I’m sure that these young pupils are going to have a fantastic experience touring around Aston Martin Head Quarters. Thank you for all the wonderful support that the Design and Technology department have had to set up this event.

A big, big thank you goes to the Design and Technology department for all the hard work and planning that went into creating the Diva Derby. I cannot wait for next year’s event which will be bigger and better.

Mr. M Williams
Head of Design and Technology
