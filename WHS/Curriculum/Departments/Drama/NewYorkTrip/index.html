New York Visit

  • back to Drama Department Homepage

 

 

Saturday began with an exciting (if sleepy) 2:30 am start, as the Drama and Music departments took to the skies with 42 students and staff, on our third visit to the Big Apple!

Arriving to an overcast New York City dampened nobody's spirits as we checked into the sophistication and comfort of our new 4-star hotel of choice, The Manhattan at Times Square, which was superbly located in the heart of the Theatre District and just a 3 minute walk from Times Square.

A visit to Macy's was followed by a great night out at the Hard Rock Cafe, which included some live 'dancing' we won't soon forget!

Sunday started briskly with a guided walking tour of sunny Midtown Manhattan, which saw us take in the famous sights of the city, including Grand Central Terminal and a walk down Fifth Avenue past Tiffany's and Trump Tower.

We finished our tour at Central Park, where a planned surprise awaited our eager travellers - ice skating at the Central Park Wollman Rink! Next was a ride on the subway as we headed downtown to catch the Staten Island Ferry, before we made our way back to get ready for the evening entertainment.

With our gladrags on, we trotted excitedly to the Schubert Theatre for an unforgettable performance of 'Matilda', which left us all, (teachers and maggots alike) totally spellbound with its amazing musical score and flawless performances from the cast and band. A pizza night at the hotel was the perfect way to relax after a busy day.

Monday propelled us to the 'Top of the Rock', giving us uninterrupted breathtaking aerial views of Manhattan, with Central Park to the North and the Empire State Building to the South.

Then it was over to Dardo Galetta Studios on 8th Ave for one of the highlights of the visit - a Broadway workshop with leading Musical Director Erica and actor Jason, who performed in last night's show! We learned the finale to "Revolting Children" and everyone threw themselves into it with enthusiasm.

A backstage tour of the legendary Radio City Music Hall completed the afternoon, before we finished the night in Times Square at Bubba Gump's for a scrummy meal.

Tuesday was a quick turnaround, as we checked out of the hotel and caught the subway downtown once more and rose to the top of the One World Trade Centre Observatory, the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere. We were greeted with unbelievable views that stretched for miles in every direction!

After visiting the 9/11 memorial fountains, we made a quick stop at the South St Sea Port, before finishing our American adventure with a trek across the Brooklyn Bridge. A brilliant end to a magical visit and well done to all of our adventuring students!
