Geraint Thomas, 2018 Tour de France winner, was given a hero’s homecoming and celebration by the thousands who gathered in Cardiff at the Senedd, and lined the streets to the Castle on Thursday afternoon 9th August.

The photographs above capture the afternoon’s celebrations on a glorious dry afternoon and early evening.

Amongst the crowds were WHS staff, students and compatriots of Geraint’s. They wanted to congratulate Geraint, former WHS student, on his Tour de France win in addition to him being a a triple World Champion, double Olympic gold medallist and Commonwealth gold medallist amongst his many incredible achievements. What an afternoon of celebration it was; organised so well by Cardiff Council and the Welsh Government.

Geraint started at the Senedd, congratulated by First Minister Carwyn Jones. He then cycled down St Mary’s Street accompanied by thirty two young representative riders from cycling clubs throughout Wales, including his former club Maindy Flyers. Geraint then rode solo up the ramp to the podium platform in front of the Castle to address his supporting fans.

The packed crowd at the Castle was swelled by national and international media personnel and the event was compered by Gethin Jones. The huge media screen displays showed extracts from the Tour de France ahead of Geraint’s arrival and the aerial shots showed just how many thousands were in attendance crowded around the Castle.

Whilst on stage Councillor Dianne Rees, Lord Mayor of the City of Cardiff, formally greeted and congratulated Geraint on behalf of the City before he answered some questions about his amazing career and this year’s Tour. Max Boyce then read his new tribute poem to Geraint called, ‘The boy who climbed a mountain’, with Geraint then presented with a signed Welsh flag by Welsh Cycling. Carwyn Jones repeated the Welsh Government's congratulations to Geraint given earlier at the Seneddd before the Welsh National Anthem was sung. Geraint gave one last long wave to the crowd before leaving the stage to meet his fans and be photographed and sign autographs.
